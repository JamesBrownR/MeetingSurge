    <p>You claimed your Google Business Profile. You filled in your hours, uploaded a few photos, and listed your services. But you left the description blank. Or you typed two sentences about being a family-owned shop with great customer service and called it done.</p>
    <p>That description field is 750 characters of free advertising that shows up every time someone considers calling you. Most shops waste it. Here's how to use it to actually get clicks.</p>

    <h2>Why the Description Actually Matters</h2>
    <p>Your GBP description does two things most shop owners don't realize.</p>
    <p>First, it influences relevance. Google reads your description to understand what you do and where you do it. If you never mention "transmission repair" in your description, Google has less reason to show you when someone searches for it. Every service you name is a ranking signal.</p>
    <p>Second, it influences clicks. When someone sees three shops in the map pack, they tap each profile to decide who to call. Your description is what they read. It either builds enough trust to make them call you, or it doesn't and they move on to the next shop.</p>
    <p>A blank description or a weak one costs you both.</p>

    <h2>What Most Auto Shop Descriptions Look Like</h2>
    <p>Here's the description that fills probably 70% of auto shop profiles:</p>
    <p><em>"We are a family-owned auto repair shop with over 20 years of experience. We are committed to providing quality service at affordable prices. We treat every customer like family. Come see us today!"</em></p>
    <p>That description says nothing specific. It names no services. It names no location. It gives the customer no reason to choose you over the shop next to you who wrote the same thing word for word.</p>
    <p>Your competitor wrote the exact same description. And neither of you is ranking for anything specific because of it.</p>

    <h2>The Formula That Actually Works</h2>
    <p>A high-performing GBP description hits four things in order:</p>
    <p><strong>1. What you do, specifically.</strong> Not "auto repair." Brake repair, transmission service, oil changes, AC repair, engine diagnostics, alignments. Name your actual services.</p>
    <p><strong>2. Where you do it.</strong> Your city and the surrounding areas you serve. Plantation, Davie, Weston, Sunrise. If someone searches in a city you serve but don't physically sit in, your description is part of what tells Google you're relevant.</p>
    <p><strong>3. Why customers trust you.</strong> ASE-certified technicians. 15 years in business. Free estimates. Same-day service. Warranty on all repairs. Pick your real differentiators and put them here.</p>
    <p><strong>4. A soft call to action.</strong> "Call us or book online." Simple. You just tell them what to do next.</p>

    <h2>A Before and After</h2>
    <p><strong>Before:</strong><br>
    <em>"Family-owned auto repair shop serving the community for over 15 years. We offer quality repairs at honest prices. Your satisfaction is our priority. Call us today!"</em></p>
    <p>That's 151 characters. Ranks for nothing. Tells the customer nothing specific.</p>
    <p><strong>After:</strong><br>
    <em>"Plantation's trusted auto repair shop serving Davie, Weston, and Sunrise since 2009. ASE-certified technicians specializing in brake repair, transmission service, oil changes, AC repair, engine diagnostics, and wheel alignments. We offer free estimates, same-day service on most repairs, and a 12-month warranty on all parts and labor. Family-owned, no upsells, no surprises. Call us to schedule or book online anytime."</em></p>
    <p>That's 484 characters. It names the location, surrounding cities, specific services, trust signals, and a clear next step. Google has plenty to work with. So does the customer reading it.</p>

    <h2>The Mistakes That Kill Your Description</h2>
    <p><strong>Keyword stuffing.</strong> "Best auto repair Plantation FL best mechanic Davie FL best oil change Weston FL." Google flags this. Customers hate reading it. Write like a human being.</p>
    <p><strong>Only talking about yourself.</strong> "We are passionate. We are committed. We care." None of that tells the customer what they actually get. Flip the focus. What do you offer them?</p>
    <p><strong>Leaving out your location.</strong> This is the single most common mistake. If your city and neighboring areas aren't in your description, you're leaving local ranking signal on the table every single day.</p>
    <p><strong>Using all 750 characters on one sentence.</strong> Break it up. Readability matters when someone is skimming your profile on a phone trying to decide whether to call you in the next 30 seconds.</p>

    <h2>How to Write Yours Right Now</h2>
    <p>Open your Google Business Profile. Go to Edit Profile. Click Business Information. Scroll to the description field.</p>
    <p>Write one sentence naming your top 5-6 services. Write one sentence naming your city and the areas around you that you serve. Write one or two sentences on your real differentiators. End with a short call to action. Read it out loud. If it sounds like a robot wrote it, rewrite it until it sounds like you.</p>
    <p>Aim for 500-700 characters. Save it. Done.</p>
    <p>That's it. One update that takes 10 minutes and immediately improves both your ranking relevance and your profile's ability to convert visitors into callers.</p>

    <h2>The Bottom Line</h2>
    <p>Your Google Business Profile description is not a formality. It's a ranking tool and a sales pitch rolled into one small text box. Most of your competitors are wasting it.</p>
    <p>Name your services. Name your city. Give customers a reason to trust you. Tell them what to do next. That's all it takes to write a description that actually does its job.</p>
