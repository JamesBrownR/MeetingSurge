Why It Matters

Each Post Is a Permanent Entry Point

A Google Business post disappears after 7 days. A blog post stays indexed indefinitely. Every post I write is a new page Google can rank for a different search, compounding your visibility month over month.

Targets Searches Your Service Pages Can't

Your service pages rank for "brake repair Plantation." Blog posts rank for "why are my brakes squeaking" and "how much does a brake job cost in Fort Lauderdale." These are searches your competitors are ignoring.

Site Speed Directly Affects Rankings

Google uses Core Web Vitals as a ranking factor. A site that loads in 4 seconds ranks below one that loads in 1.5, even with identical content. Regular speed optimization keeps you competitive as Google updates its standards.

Security Problems Kill Rankings

A hacked or flagged site gets deindexed fast. Google won't send users to a site it considers unsafe. Regular security updates and monitoring prevent problems before they cost you rankings and customers.

Content Converts Before Customers Call

Someone who reads your article on "signs your brakes need replacing" before they call already trusts you. They're not price shopping. They found you through useful information and you're already the expert in their mind.

Authority Compounds Over Time

A shop that publishes one post a month for a year has 12 indexed pages, each targeting a different search. Two years in, that's 24 entry points from Google. The shops building this now will be increasingly hard to displace later.
